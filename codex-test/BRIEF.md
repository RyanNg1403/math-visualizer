# Task brief

Build a complete visual explainer for **Taylor series** — how polynomials can approximate any smooth function near a point, term by term — for a viewer who is comfortable with functions, graphs, and the idea of a derivative, but has never seen a Taylor series.

Deliver **both phases** of the math-deck-video skill:

1. The interactive HTML slide deck (`taylor-deck.html` in this directory), fully verified per the skill's Gate 2 checklist.
2. The rendered video (`taylor.mp4` in this directory): Motion Canvas port, 1080p60, with the generated ambient music bed, verified per Gates 4-5.

Suggested money shot (yours to refine): terms adding one at a time — constant, then slope, then curvature — with the polynomial visibly hugging sin(x) or e^x further and further from the center point, plus the failure case (moving far from the expansion point, or a function like 1/(1-x) outside its radius).

When done, write `REPORT.md` summarizing: scene list, what was verified with evidence paths, what was not verified, and any deviations from the skill with reasons.
